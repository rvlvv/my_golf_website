About My Golf Journey

From the moment I first gripped a weathered 7-iron at age six, I was hooked. My weekends transformed into sun-kissed mornings on emerald fairways—chasing tiny white balls across dew-laden grass. By ten I was in junior tournaments, my heart pounding with every putt. The early losses taught me resilience; the occasional triumphs taught me humility. Over the years, I sharpened my slice into a draw, practiced bunker shots beneath endless skies, and learned that golf is as much about patience as power. Each tournament—neighborhood club events or regional opens—added a new chapter, full of mishits and birdie celebrations that felt like winning the Masters. Golf has been my classroom: teaching focus, sportsmanship, and the joy of chasing perfection even when it’s perfectly out of reach.
